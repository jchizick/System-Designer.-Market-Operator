A robust system survives its operator's worst days. If it requires constant intervention, it isn't a system.

The point of a system is not to remove judgment. It is to prevent judgment from being hijacked by fatigue, urgency, or emotional debt from the last decision.

Robustness shows up in the boring parts. Clear thresholds. Repeatable inputs. Defined failure states. Fewer places where mood can rewrite the process.

The best systems assume the operator will sometimes be tired, impatient, distracted, or overconfident. They are designed with that reality in mind.

If the process only works when everything is perfect, it is not operational. It is decorative.
