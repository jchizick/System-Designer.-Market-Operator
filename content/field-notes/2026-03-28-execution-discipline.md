Most traders don't fail from lack of knowledge. They fail from lack of execution discipline.

The market does not care how much theory sits behind the setup. It only exposes whether the operator can follow the plan when speed, loss, and ego enter the room.

Execution discipline is the ability to reduce the trade back to its rules. Entry conditions. Risk size. Invalidation. Exit behavior. No negotiation after the signal fires.

A system is only real when it survives pressure. If the operator changes the rules every time the tape gets loud, the edge never gets a clean sample size.

The work is not to feel calm. The work is to act correctly while the nervous system is trying to interfere.
