Cognitive load is not just a user problem.

It is a design problem.

Every unclear layout, vague label, unnecessary step, and competing visual priority taxes the operator.

The best systems do not make users feel smart because they survived complexity.

They make users feel sharp because the next move is obvious.

Clarity is not simplification for its own sake.

Clarity is compression.

It turns complexity into something usable.
